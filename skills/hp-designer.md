# Skill: HP Designer

> Design improvements to the B8E website based on Reader output.
> This skill proposes only. It does not implement.

---

## Purpose

Take the structured analysis from `hp-reader.md` and produce a clear, philosophy-aligned improvement design — covering structure, messaging, and CTA — ready for guardian review.

This output becomes the input for `hp-guardian.md`.

---

## Activation

Triggered when the user provides a completed Reader Mode analysis and requests a redesign or improvement.

---

## Reference Files

Always read before designing:
```
docs/b8e-philosophy.md     — Core principles (non-negotiable)
docs/writing-principles.md — Language and copy guidelines
docs/site-structure.md     — Current structure and CSS classes
```

---

## Design Principles

### Being over Doing
Lead with presence and philosophy. Services and features follow.

### Resonance over Persuasion
The reader should feel "this is for me" without being told.
No claims, no urgency, no social proof pressure.

### Natural Flow over Forced Structure
Each section exists because it creates meaning — not to fill space.
Rhythm and silence are part of the design.

### Space as Intent
Whitespace is not absence. It is where the reader breathes and decides.

### The CTA is a Door, Not a Push
Contact pathways are quiet, timeless, and open.
The reader moves when ready — not when pressured.

---

## Design Process

1. **Identify the root intention** of the page or section
2. **Restate it as a Being statement** (not a service statement)
3. **Design the flow** from that intention outward:
   - What does the reader feel upon arrival?
   - What question does this page hold for them?
   - Where does it naturally end?
4. **Propose messaging** that matches the tone in `docs/writing-principles.md`
5. **Design the CTA** as the quietest possible invitation

---

## Output Format

Always produce output in this exact structure:

```
1. Concept
   — The single guiding intention for this improvement

2. New Structure
   — Full page or section composition
   — Show relationship to current structure

3. Section Design
   — Per-section: purpose, content direction, design notes

4. Messaging Examples
   — Hero copy, section leads, resonance questions, CTA text
   — Mark as [example] — not final copy

5. CTA Design
   — Tone, placement, link target, visual treatment
```

---

## Rules

- Do not implement. Propose only.
- Do not add new content — refine and reframe existing content when possible
- Every proposal must pass the Philosophy Compliance Check before output:

```
[ ] Does this create resonance or pressure?
[ ] Is the language Being-centered or Doing-centered?
[ ] Does the CTA invite or push?
[ ] Is there enough space for the reader to breathe?
[ ] Would a reader feel "this is for me" — or "I am being sold to"?
```

If any answer is misaligned, revise before presenting.
